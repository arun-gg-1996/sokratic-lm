### Key observations

1. **threshold=95 is the only one that passes Gate 1**, but produces the worst
   chunk quality — mean 1,366 chars, 43% of chunks over 900 chars, max 16,649.
   Long chunks hurt proposition extraction quality (Claude misses buried facts).

2. **threshold=85 has the best balance**: mean and median in the target band,
   max under 10K, passes Gate 1 with only a 415-chunk overage. The Gate 1
   limit of 3,000 was set conservatively before proposition expansion was
   fully accounted for.

3. **threshold=75 produces research-ideal chunk sizes** (mean 531, close to
   DPR 100-word passages) but doubles the proposition count to ~12,000.

### Recommendation

**Use threshold=85** and raise the Gate 1 upper bound from 3,000 → 4,000.

Rationale:
- The 3,000 limit was a conservative first estimate, not derived from downstream
  Qdrant or retrieval constraints
- Qdrant handles 10K–100K vectors trivially
- Better chunk quality (mean 749, max 6,022) will improve proposition extraction
  and retrieval precision compared to threshold=95's oversized chunks
- 415-chunk overage is minor; the limit should be adjusted to match the actual
  architecture, not the other way around

If keeping Gate 1 at 3,000 is a hard requirement, threshold=95 (1,886 chunks)
is the only option that passes — but accept the tradeoff of lower chunk quality.
