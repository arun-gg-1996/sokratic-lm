## Two final bugs from suite run #4 fixed; suite is clean

### Bug A1 — `_prelock_refuse_call` LLM leaked the answer

**Symptom**: in suite run #3 + #4, A1 turn 2 produced a "refuse with
cards" message that included *"try a term like 'sinoatrial node' or
'cardiac conduction system'"*. The Dean LLM was being helpfully
specific in its refuse copy, but `sinoatrial node` IS the locked
answer we're trying to keep the student from being told.

**Root cause**: the prompt at `config/base.yaml:751`
(`dean_prelock_refuse_static`) said *"invite them to try one of the
fresh options OR name a more specific term."* — the "specific term"
phrasing implicitly invited the LLM to suggest one. No explicit
prohibition against naming anatomical terms.

**Fix**: added a `HARD RULE — NO TERM SUGGESTIONS` section to the
prompt with concrete forbidden examples (`"try a term like 'X'"`,
`"you could search for 'X'"`, `"the term you're looking for is
probably 'X'"`) and concrete allowed alternatives (`"Pick one of the
cards below, or rephrase your question"`, `"Try a different angle —
maybe a different body system"`).

Verified post-fix: turn 2 now produces *"A few of your questions
haven't lined up with what's well-covered in our material — let's
get you on solid ground. Pick one of the cards below, or try
rephrasing your question from a different angle."* No anatomical
terms named.

### Bug G1 — Whitespace-only input → Anthropic API 400

**Symptom**: suite run #3 + #4 produced
`BadRequestError: messages: text content blocks must contain
non-whitespace text` when the student sent `"   "` (whitespace).
Crashed the scenario.

**Root cause**: input sanitization gap. We accepted whitespace-only
content into `state["messages"]` and passed it through to the
Anthropic messages.create payload, which rejects empty content
blocks.

**Fix**: input guard at the top of `dean_node`
(`conversation/nodes.py:172`). If the latest student message strips
to empty, skip the LLM path entirely and return a friendly retry
message: *"Looks like your last message came through empty — could
you type your question or response again?"*. Logged as
`wrapper="dean_node.whitespace_input_guard"` in turn_trace for
forensics.

### A1 scenario itself was also subtly wrong

The 3-turn version assumed reach=True would fire on turn 3 when the
student said *"The SA node — the sinoatrial node."*. But that turn
IS the topic-lock turn — `topic_just_locked=True` deliberately
suppresses the reach gate (architectural design — students often
say *"let's learn about X"* when picking the topic, not *"the answer
is X"*; the system avoids false-firing reach on the topic-pick
utterance).

Fix: added a 4th turn where the student re-asserts the answer
*"The sinoatrial node — that's what initiates the heartbeat."*. On
this turn the topic is already locked, the gate runs, and reach=True
fires correctly via Step A token overlap.

This is the right architectural-vs-test split: the system's behavior
is correct (don't count the topic-pick as a reach attempt); the test
just needed an extra post-lock turn.
